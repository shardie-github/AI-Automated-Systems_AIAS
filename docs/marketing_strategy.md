## Free vs. Paid Content Gating

### Free Content (No Gate)

**Purpose**: Build trust, provide value, drive awareness
**Content Types**:
- Blog posts (all)
- Basic tutorials
- Free templates (limited)
- Case study previews
- Help center articles
- Basic frameworks

**Conversion Strategy**:
- Soft CTAs: "Start free trial", "Learn more"
- Newsletter signup
- Social sharing

### Email Gate (Free with Email)

**Purpose**: Lead generation, email list building
**Content Types**:
- Advanced templates
- Detailed case studies
- Comprehensive guides (PDF)
- Webinar recordings
- Tool downloads

**Conversion Strategy**:
- Email capture form
- Value proposition: "Get free [resource]"
- Post-download email sequence

### Trial Gate (Free Trial Required)

**Purpose**: Drive trial signups, early conversion
**Content Types**:
- Interactive tools (ROI calculator)
- Advanced templates
- Workflow builder access
- Limited analytics

**Conversion Strategy**:
- "Start free trial" CTA
- Trial benefits messaging
- No credit card required

### Paid Gate (Subscription Required)

**Purpose**: Drive paid conversions, provide premium value
**Content Types**:
- Full case studies with step-by-step solutions
- Advanced frameworks
- Premium templates
- Advanced analytics
- Personalized insights
- Onboarding sessions

**Conversion Strategy**:
- "Upgrade to unlock" messaging
- Clear value proposition
- Feature comparison
- Social proof

### Content Gating Rules

#### Always Free:
- Blog posts
- Basic tutorials
- Help center
- Product documentation
- Basic templates (3-5)

#### Email Gate:
- Advanced templates (10+)
- Detailed case studies
- Comprehensive guides
- Webinar recordings

#### Trial Gate:
- Interactive tools
- Workflow builder
- Limited analytics
- Basic personalization

#### Paid Gate:
- Full case studies
- Premium templates (50+)
- Advanced analytics
- Personalized insights
- Onboarding sessions

## Social Media Strategy

- **Audience**: B2B professionals, decision-makers
- **Content**: Thought leadership, case studies, industry insights
- **Frequency**: 3-5 posts per week
- **Best Times**: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM, 12-1 PM

#### Twitter/X (Secondary)
- **Audience**: Tech-savvy professionals, automation enthusiasts
- **Content**: Quick tips, news, product updates
- **Frequency**: 5-7 posts per week
- **Best Times**: Weekdays, 9-10 AM, 1-2 PM

#### Facebook (Tertiary)
- **Audience**: Small business owners, entrepreneurs
- **Content**: Case studies, tutorials, community engagement
- **Frequency**: 2-3 posts per week
- **Best Times**: Weekdays, 1-3 PM

## Content Production Workflow

### Content Creation Process

1. **Ideation** (Weekly)
   - Content calendar review
   - Keyword research
   - Topic brainstorming
   - Competitor analysis

2. **Planning** (Weekly)
   - Outline creation
   - Keyword integration
   - Internal linking plan
   - CTA placement

3. **Creation** (As scheduled)
   - Writing
   - Editing
   - SEO optimization
   - Image creation

4. **Review** (Before publishing)
   - Quality check
   - SEO audit
   - Link verification
   - CTA testing

5. **Publishing** (Scheduled)
   - Publish to blog
   - Social media promotion
   - Email newsletter inclusion
   - Internal linking update

6. **Promotion** (Post-publish)
   - Social media posts
   - Email newsletter
   - Community sharing
   - Paid promotion (if applicable)

### Content Templates

#### Blog Post Template:
- Title (keyword-optimized)
- Meta description
- Introduction (hook, value proposition)
- Main content (H2s, H3s, bullet points)
- Conclusion (summary, CTA)
- Internal links (3-5)
- CTA (trial signup, newsletter)

#### Case Study Template:
- Title (company + result)
- Challenge
- Solution
- Results (metrics, quotes)
- Key takeaways
- CTA (trial, upgrade)

#### Tutorial Template:
- Title (how-to format)
- Introduction (what you'll learn)
- Prerequisites
- Step-by-step instructions
- Tips & best practices
- Troubleshooting
- CTA (related content, trial)
