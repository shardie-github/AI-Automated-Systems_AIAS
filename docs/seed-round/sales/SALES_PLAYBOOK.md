### Firmographics:
- **Company Size**: 10-500 employees
- **Revenue**: $1M-$50M annually
- **Industry**:

  - SaaS
  - Professional Services

  - Mid-Market Businesses

  - Shopify, Stripe, Slack, Gmail
  - Multiple disconnected tools
  - Manual processes between tools

  - Manual, repetitive tasks
  - Tools that don't integrate

  - Complaining about manual processes

- **Primary**: Operations Manager, CTO, VP Operations
- **Secondary**: CEO, Founder, CFO
- **Influencers**: Team leads, power users

## SALES METHODOLOGY

### Framework: MEDDIC

**M - Metrics**: What metrics matter to the customer?
**E - Economic Buyer**: Who has budget authority?
**D - Decision Criteria**: What are their evaluation criteria?
**D - Decision Process**: How do they make decisions?
**I - Identify Pain**: What's their biggest pain point?
**C - Champion**: Who will advocate for us internally?

### Sales Approach: Consultative Selling

1. **Understand** their business and challenges
2. **Educate** on automation best practices
3. **Demonstrate** value through specific use cases
4. **Collaborate** on solution design
5. **Close** with clear ROI

## SALES STAGES & ACTIVITIES

### Stage 1: Prospecting

**Goal**: Identify and contact potential customers

**Activities**:
- [ ] Research target companies (LinkedIn, company websites)
- [ ] Identify decision makers
- [ ] Craft personalized outreach
- [ ] Send initial email/cold call
- [ ] Follow up (3-5 touches)

**Success Criteria**: Meeting scheduled

**Tools**: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Outreach.io, HubSpot

### Stage 2: Qualification

**Goal**: Determine if prospect is a good fit

**Activities**:
- [ ] BANT Qualification (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)
- [ ] ICP fit assessment
- [ ] Pain point identification
- [ ] Decision maker identification

**Success Criteria**: Qualified lead (BANT + ICP fit)

**Tools**: Qualification checklist, CRM

### Stage 3: Discovery

**Goal**: Understand customer's specific needs

**Activities**:
- [ ] Discovery call (30-45 minutes)
- [ ] Map current processes
- [ ] Identify automation opportunities
- [ ] Understand technical requirements
- [ ] Assess integration needs

**Success Criteria**: Clear understanding of needs, use cases identified

**Tools**: Discovery question framework, process mapping

### Stage 4: Demo

**Goal**: Demonstrate value through specific use cases

**Activities**:
- [ ] Prepare customized demo
- [ ] Show relevant workflows
- [ ] Address specific use cases
- [ ] Handle objections
- [ ] Confirm interest

**Success Criteria**: Positive response, next steps agreed

**Tools**: Demo environment, screen sharing, battlecards

### Stage 5: Proposal

**Goal**: Present formal proposal with pricing

**Activities**:
- [ ] Create customized proposal
- [ ] Include ROI calculation
- [ ] Present pricing and terms
- [ ] Address concerns
- [ ] Set timeline for decision

**Success Criteria**: Proposal sent, decision timeline set

**Tools**: Proposal template, ROI calculator

### Stage 6: Negotiation

**Goal**: Finalize terms and address concerns

**Activities**:
- [ ] Address pricing concerns
- [ ] Negotiate terms
- [ ] Handle final objections
- [ ] Provide references (if needed)
- [ ] Finalize contract terms

**Success Criteria**: Terms agreed, contract ready

**Tools**: Contract template, pricing flexibility matrix

### Stage 7: Close

**Goal**: Execute contract and onboard customer

**Activities**:
- [ ] Send contract for signature
- [ ] Follow up on signature
- [ ] Process payment
- [ ] Hand off to Customer Success
- [ ] Schedule onboarding

**Success Criteria**: Contract signed, payment received

**Tools**: DocuSign, payment processing, CRM

## QUALIFICATION FRAMEWORK

### BANT Qualification

**B - Budget**:
- Do they have budget for this?
- What's their budget range?
- Who controls the budget?
- **Qualified**: Yes/No

**A - Authority**:
- Are we talking to the decision maker?
- Who else is involved in the decision?
- What's their role in the decision?
- **Qualified**: Yes/No

**N - Need**:
- Do they have a clear need/pain point?
- Is it urgent?
- What happens if they don't solve it?
- **Qualified**: Yes/No

**T - Timeline**:
- When do they need to solve this?
- What's driving the timeline?
- When can they make a decision?
- **Qualified**: Yes/No

**Overall Qualification**: All 4 must be "Yes"

### ICP Fit Assessment

- [ ] Company size: 10-500 employees
- [ ] Industry: Target industry
- [ ] Current tools: Using relevant tools
- [ ] Pain points: Match our solution
- [ ] Budget: Can afford our pricing
- [ ] Technical resources: Limited (our sweet spot)

**Qualified**: 5+ checkmarks

## DISCOVERY QUESTIONS

### Opening Questions:
1. "Tell me about your role and what you're responsible for."
2. "What does a typical day/week look like for you?"
3. "What are your biggest priorities right now?"

### Problem Discovery:
4. "What manual or repetitive tasks do you handle regularly?"
5. "How much time do you spend on these tasks?"
6. "What happens if these tasks don't get done?"
7. "Have you tried to automate any of these processes? What happened?"

### Current State:
8. "What tools or systems do you use to manage your work?"
9. "How well do these tools work together?"
10. "What's missing or frustrating about your current setup?"

### Solution Validation:
11. "If you could wave a magic wand and automate one process, what would it be?"
12. "What would you do with the time you'd save?"
13. "How much would that be worth to you?"

### Decision Process:
14. "How do you typically evaluate and purchase new tools?"
15. "Who else is involved in this decision?"
16. "What's your timeline for making a decision?"
17. "What would need to be true for you to move forward?"

## DEMO SCRIPT

### Pre-Demo Preparation:
- [ ] Review discovery notes
- [ ] Identify 2-3 specific use cases
- [ ] Prepare relevant workflows
- [ ] Test demo environment
- [ ] Prepare screen sharing

### Demo Structure (30 minutes):

**1. Introduction (2 min)**
- Thank them for their time
- Confirm agenda and timeline
- Set expectations: "I'll show you how AIAS Platform can solve [specific use case]"

**2. Problem Recap (3 min)**
- "Based on our conversation, you mentioned [problem]"
- "Let me show you how we solve that"

**3. Platform Overview (5 min)**
- Dashboard walkthrough
- Key features overview
- Mobile app (if relevant)

**4. Use Case Demo (15 min)**
- **Use Case 1**: [Their specific use case]
  - Show workflow builder
  - Demonstrate trigger and actions
  - Show results
- **Use Case 2**: [Additional relevant use case]
  - Similar walkthrough

**5. Q&A (3 min)**
- Address questions
- Handle objections
- Confirm interest

**6. Next Steps (2 min)**
- "Based on what you've seen, what are your thoughts?"
- "What would you need to move forward?"
- Schedule next steps

### Demo Best Practices:
- **Show, Don't Tell**: Demonstrate, don't just describe
- **Use Their Language**: Use their terminology
- **Focus on Value**: Tie features to their specific needs
- **Handle Objections**: Address concerns immediately
- **Confirm Interest**: Gauge their reaction throughout

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### Common Objections & Responses:

**"We're not ready for automation."**
- **Response**: "Many customers say that, but automation actually helps you get ready. Start small with one workflow, see the value, then expand. What's one process you'd like to automate?"

**"We don't have budget."**
- **Response**: "I understand budget constraints. Let's calculate the ROI. If we save you [X] hours per week, that's [Y] hours per year. At [Z] per hour, that's [Value] in savings. Our platform pays for itself. Would a Starter tier at $99/month work?"

**"We need to think about it."**
- **Response**: "Of course. What specifically would you like to think about? Is there information I can provide to help with your decision?"

**"We're evaluating other options."**
- **Response**: "That makes sense. What criteria are you using to evaluate? I'd love to show you how AIAS Platform compares on those criteria."

**"We need custom features."**
- **Response**: "What specific features do you need? Many customers find our platform handles 90% of their needs. For the remaining 10%, we offer custom integrations and white-label options."

See [Sales Battlecards](./SALES_BATTLECARDS.md) for detailed objection handling.

## CLOSING TECHNIQUES

### Assumptive Close:
- "When would you like to get started? We can have you up and running by [date]."

### Alternative Close:
- "Would you prefer to start with the Starter tier at $99/month, or go straight to Pro at $499/month?"

### Summary Close:
- "Based on everything we've discussed, it sounds like AIAS Platform would save you [X] hours per week and [Y] dollars per year. Does that sound right?"

### Urgency Close:
- "We're offering early-bird pricing for customers who commit this month. That's a 20% discount. Would that help with your decision?"

### Trial Close:
- "Would you like to start with a 14-day free trial? No credit card required. You can see the value before committing."
